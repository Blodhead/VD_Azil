The first congress on rescue animals in Serbia

The first congress on animal protection in Serbia, held on September 16, 2017, at the Radisson Collection Hotel, Old Mill Belgrade, 15 Vojvode Mišića Boulevard, ended in a constructive tone, with the belief that the next one will be even better. The congress was opened by Ivana Lazić, head of the Welfare Department, Veterinary Directorate of the Ministry of Agriculture, Forestry and Water Management. There were about 30 participants, 12 lecturers, 2 exhibitors, 4 media representatives and ARS volunteers.

Representatives of the Natural History Museum, the Institute for Biological Research, the Faculty of Veterinary Medicine, the Institute for Nature Protection of Serbia, the Kennel Club of Serbia, the Palic Zoo, the Faculty of Agriculture, DZPPPS, BAK, IN AKT, Orca, Phoenix, Azil Staro Brdo, Earth Era and many other individuals.

We are glad that the congress, although the first, gained an international character with the participation of Jenny Brown, the founder of WFS, and we hope that in the future there will be more lecturers and participants from abroad. It was concluded that more mutual cooperation and better connection between the governmental and non-governmental sectors is needed. To this end, the participants agreed that it is necessary to continue and expand our mutual communication, understanding and, wherever possible, joint work on the protection and improvement of the position of animals in Serbia. Also, all lecturers emphasized the need to involve the media more and continuously work on educating citizens and all those involved in animal protection.

 
 

Lea the dachshund adopted!

In February, the 5-year-old shelter pup from Kent, England, earned the title "heartbreaker" when he made headlines for getting stood up at his Valentine's Day-themed adoption party. Before the poorly-attended event, Lea had spent over 200 days at the Battersea Brands Hatch shelter.

Lea's story of being unlucky in love touched many who felt "shattered," according to a release from Battersea, about the dog throwing a party where nobody came. News of the pup's bad luck spread worldwide, leading Battersea Brands Hatch to receive mountains of mail from Lea's growing fanbase. Many of the dog's admirers sent him toys, handwritten love notes, donations, and letters expressing their interest in bringing the canine home

Karl and Jill Kendrick of Derbyshire, England, were among the many who inquired about adopting Lea home after learning that the dog got stood up at his Valentine's Day adoption party. After learning about the Kendricks, Battersea Brands Hatch found that the couple, and their two daughters, would make the ideal forever family for Lea.

"How Lea stayed in the care of Battersea for so long is a mystery to me, any new owner would have been lucky to rehome him. Since we've brought him home, he's shown himself to be the perfect dog and has really settled in so quickly — we already love him so much and wouldn't change him for the world," Karl Kendrick said in a statement about welcoming Lea into the family.
